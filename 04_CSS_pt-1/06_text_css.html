Jeff the Land Shark (also known as Jeffrey) is a fictional character who appears in American comic books published by Marvel Comics. Created by writer Kelly Thompson and artist Daniele di Nicuolo, the character first appeared in West Coast Avengers (vol. 3) #6 (October 2018), but was not named and formally introduced until issue #7 (January 2019). Jeff is the pet landshark (legged shark) of the superheroes Gwen Poole and Kate Bishop, and has also been cared for by Wade Wilson and Elsa Bloodstone.

He has subsequently appeared in a variety of additional media. He headlines in the digital Marvel's Infinity Comics series It's Jeff! written by Thompson and drawn by Gurihiru, which was first published in September 2021. This vertical scrolling format comic returned for a second season in September 2022, a third season in October 2023, and a fourth season in November 2024. At the Eisner Awards, It's Jeff! was nominated in 2022 for Best Digital Comic and won in 2024 for Best Humor Publication. In December 2024, Jeff debuted as a playable character in the video game Marvel Rivals. Jeff headlines in the print limited series Jeff The Land Shark (vol. 1), written by Thompson and illustrated by Tokitokoro, which began in June 2025.

Publication History

Comic Books

Jeff the Land Shark debuted officially in West Coast Avengers #7 (2019), created by Kelly Thompson and Daniele di Nicuolo. Landsharks first appeared in issue #1 (2018), and Jeff was inspired by one of the designs along with Thompson's cats. Originally part of a running gag where Gwen Poole adopted and named various animals "Jeff," the character quickly gained popularity and became a recurring part of the series. Jeff returned in titles like Gwenpool Strikes Back, Deadpool (vol. 8), and Marvel #1000.

Jeff’s popularity grew thanks to fan art by Gurihiru, which led to more appearances and even in-universe merchandise. He became the star of It's Jeff!, a silent comedy digital comic launched in 2021 on Marvel Unlimited, written by Thompson and drawn by Gurihiru. The series has had four seasons and several print collections, including It's Jeff! #1 and The Jeff-Verse #1. Jeff also appeared in Marvel Meow, Infinity Paws, Extreme Venomverse, and Venom War: It's Jeff!.

Jeff continued to appear in digital series like Jeff Week and Marvel Rivals Infinity Comic (2024), and guest-starred in Gwenpool (2025) with Kate Bishop. He now headlines his own limited print series, Jeff the Land Shark (2025), and is set to appear with Krypto in Deadpool/Batman #1, a Marvel/DC crossover.

Covers

Jeff has been featured on several variant covers, including Captain Marvel #39, #42, and Captain America: Symbol of Truth #6, all illustrated by Gurihiru. Nao Fuji also included Jeff on a Shang-Chi and the Ten Rings #4 variant. A venomized version of Jeff appeared on two Extreme Venomverse #5 covers by Leinil Francis Yu and Peach Momoko.

Fictional Character Biography

The land sharks were created by MODOK Superior, and after his defeat by the West Coast Avengers, Gwen Poole adopted a baby land shark and named him Jeff—after a kitten she previously had to give up. Gwen, who has a shark-themed aesthetic, including a shark backpack and phone case, added Jeff to the West Coast Avengers. Later, fearing her own declining presence in comics would affect Jeff’s future, she temporarily entrusted him to Deadpool, whose title was then canceled, and briefly to Elsa Bloodstone.

In It's Jeff! (2021), Jeff is shown living again with Gwen and Kate Bishop. The series humorously depicts him sledding with Captain America's shield, eating the Infinity Gauntlet, and stealing Thanksgiving dinner. Gwen returns in Season 2 to continue co-parenting Jeff with Kate. During the Venom War storyline, Jeff bonds with a Symbiote, helps fight off a Zombiote invasion alongside Kate, Gwen, America Chavez, and Fuse, and later celebrates their victory with churros.

Powers and Abilities

Jeff is a small, four-legged land shark with an insatiable appetite. His primary ability is biting, and he exhibits typical shark traits. To disguise himself in public, he often wears a variety of cute animal costumes.
